# Dimension 4: Business Viability Risk

Does this work for the business?

## Criteria

- **B1: Business Model Alignment** — Does this align with the current business model and strategy?
- **B2: Legal Compliance** — Are legal and regulatory concerns addressed?
- **B3: Unit Economics** — Are unit economics (cost to build, cost to serve, expected revenue) validated?
- **B4: Operationalization** — Can sales, marketing, and support operationalize this?
- **B5: Competitive Position** — Does this create a sustainable competitive advantage?

## Proposal Questions

1. How does this align with the business model? (B1)
2. Are there legal or compliance concerns? Has legal been consulted? (B2)
3. What are the unit economics — cost to build, cost to serve, expected revenue? (B3)
4. Can sales position it, marketing message it, and support handle it? (B4)
5. Does this create a sustainable competitive advantage? How defensible? (B5)

## Implementation — Compliance Questions

1. Were business constraints evaluated before building? (B1)
2. Was legal consulted for regulated domains? (B2)
3. Was a go-to-market plan defined? (B4)

## Implementation — Results Questions

1. Is it profitable or sustainable at current scale? (B3)
2. Have legal issues surfaced post-launch? (B2)
3. Is sales able to position and sell it? (B4)
4. Are unit economics holding at current volume? (B3)

## Scoring

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Unit economics validated; legal complete; GTM in place; competitive moat documented |
| 4 | Business case solid with minor gaps (e.g., competitive moat loosely defined) |
| 3 | Business case sketched but not validated with data |
| 2 | Assumed viable without analysis; "we'll monetize later" |
| 1 | No business analysis; viability not considered |

## Quality Check

A score of 4+ requires at minimum: validated unit economics, legal sign-off for regulated domains, and a documented GTM plan.

---

## Extended Criteria

### B6: Sales Enablement

Can go-to-market teams effectively position, sell, and support this?

- **Training lift**: What training do sales, support, and customer success teams need? How long before they're effective?
- **Tooling**: Are sales collateral, demo environments, pricing calculators, and competitive battle cards prepared?
- **Process changes**: Does this require new sales motions (e.g., PLG to enterprise, self-serve to assisted)?
- **Support readiness**: Can support resolve customer issues without engineering escalation?

**Scoring impact:** For B2B products, B6 gaps can cap the Viability score at 3 even if B1-B5 are strong.

**Cross-reference:** See [frameworks-business-models.md](frameworks-business-models.md) for model-specific viability guidance — SaaS, marketplace, platform, enterprise, and PLG products each have distinct viability risk profiles.
