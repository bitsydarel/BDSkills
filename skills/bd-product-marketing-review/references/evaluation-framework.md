# Evaluation Framework

Detailed scoring criteria for the 10 PMM review dimensions.

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 5 | Excellent | Framework-complete, evidence-rich, no gaps |
| 4 | Good | Framework applied with minor gaps |
| 3 | Adequate | Partially addressed; notable methodology gaps |
| 2 | Weak | Significant gaps; ad-hoc approach |
| 1 | Missing | Not addressed or fundamentally flawed |

## Dimension 1: Market Understanding & Buyer Personas

**Proposal questions**: Is the problem clearly stated using the 4 U's framework (Unworkable, Unavoidable, Urgent, Underserved)? Are buyer personas validated beyond demographics? Is the decision-making unit mapped (economic buyer, champion, technical evaluator, blocker)? Are JTBD identified across all three dimensions (functional, social, emotional)? Is there evidence of workarounds observed in the field? Is the market sized (TAM/SAM/SOM)?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Were buyer personas validated before launch? Was the decision-making unit mapped? Was market sizing conducted? Was the problem assessed for noteworthiness?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Does post-launch data confirm the problem was real? Are buyers engaging as the persona model predicted? Does the TAM/SAM/SOM estimate hold against actual pipeline data?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Buyer personas validated at Level 5 (win/loss validated). Problem assessed using 4 U's framework (Unworkable + Unavoidable + Urgent + Underserved). JTBD mapped with functional, social, and emotional dimensions. Evidence of workarounds observed in the field. Problem framed using 4 W's (Who, What, Where, Why). TAM/SAM/SOM sized with methodology. Decision-making unit fully mapped.
- **4**: Level 4 personas (buying behaviors). Problem validated as noteworthy on 3 of 4 U's. JTBD identified but emotional/social dimensions thin. Workarounds documented. Market sizing present.
- **3**: Level 3 personas (behavioral). Problem described but not rigorously assessed — team cannot articulate whether the problem is Blatant or Latent. No JTBD analysis. Limited market sizing.
- **2**: Level 2 personas (demographic only). Problem assumed, not validated. No decision-making unit mapping. No evidence of customer workarounds observed.
- **1**: No buyer persona work. Problem is an internal assumption. Target market is "everyone." No market sizing.

**Problem noteworthiness check**: Apply the 4 U's — Is this problem Unworkable (broken process with measurable consequences)? Unavoidable (all participants in the target segment encounter it)? Urgent (top-3 priority, active budget allocation)? Underserved (current solutions are inadequate, evidenced by workarounds)? A problem scoring high on all 4 U's is a strong market signal. Also check: Is the problem Blatant and Critical (customers actively searching for solutions to a mission-critical issue) or Latent and Aspirational (customers unaware or only mildly interested)? Blatant+Critical = strong PMM signal; Latent+Aspirational = requires significant market education investment.

## Dimension 2: Positioning & Messaging Clarity

**Proposal questions**: Has the team completed Dunford's 5-component positioning process? Are competitive alternatives identified from buyer interviews (not internal assumptions)? Are unique attributes evidence-backed? Are 2-4 value themes mapped to buyer outcomes? Are target customer characteristics validated? Is the market category deliberately chosen? Is messaging tested with target buyers?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Was Dunford's framework applied before launch? Were all 5 components completed? Was messaging tested with buyers, not just internal review?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Does the sales team confirm prospects "immediately get it"? Does win/loss data confirm positioning resonance? Is messaging consistent across all touchpoints?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: All 5 Dunford components completed (competitive alternatives from buyer interviews, unique attributes with evidence, 2-4 value themes, target customer characteristics validated, market category deliberately chosen). Messaging tested with 5+ target buyers. Sales team confirms prospects "immediately get it."
- **4**: Framework applied but one component is thin (e.g., competitive alternatives include phantom competitors, or market category is default rather than deliberate).
- **3**: Some positioning exists but lacks structural rigor. Positioning is descriptive rather than strategic — statements like "we are a platform that does X, Y, Z" instead of framing competitive alternatives and value themes.
- **2**: Feature-list positioning. No competitive context. No target customer definition. No market category choice.
- **1**: No positioning work done. Product described by what it does technically, not why buyers should care.

**Dunford trap detection**: Check for Default Positioning (inherited, not deliberate), Phantom Competitors (never actually lose to them), Status Quo Blindness (20-30% of enterprise deals lost to "no decision"), Impossible New Market (creating a category when subsegment would work), Forced Trend (layering a trend with no product connection).

## Dimension 3: Competitive Differentiation

**Proposal questions**: Are competitive alternatives identified (including status quo and "do nothing")? Is differentiation defensible (not just feature-based)? Has the category strategy been chosen deliberately (head-to-head, subsegment, or new category)? Are battle cards created? Is the competitive intelligence program active?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Were competitive alternatives assessed before launch? Was the category strategy deliberate? Were battle cards distributed to sales?

**Implementation-Results questions**: What does win/loss data show about competitive positioning? Are deals being lost to expected or unexpected competitors? Is the differentiation holding in market?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Competitive alternatives identified from buyer interviews (including status quo). Category strategy deliberately chosen with rationale. Differentiation defensible and not feature-dependent. Battle cards created, distributed, and updated quarterly. Win/loss data informs competitive strategy.
- **4**: Competitive analysis present but one area thin (e.g., status quo not assessed, or battle cards created but not updated).
- **3**: Some competitive awareness but no structured analysis. Differentiation is feature-based and easily copied. No category strategy decision.
- **2**: Competitors listed but not analyzed. No battle cards. No understanding of why deals are won or lost.
- **1**: No competitive analysis. Team unaware of competitive alternatives. No differentiation articulated.

## Dimension 4: Go-to-Market Strategy & Execution

**Proposal questions**: Is there a GTM motion matched to buyer complexity and ACV? Does the strategy cover both halves of the bow-tie (acquisition AND expansion)? Is the MOVE framework's stage-appropriate approach applied (Ideation/Transition/Execution)? Are funnel metrics defined at each stage with conversion and velocity targets?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Was a GTM motion selected and documented? Was the bow-tie model applied? Was the MOVE stage identified?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Is pipeline being generated as planned? Are both acquisition AND expansion metrics healthy? Is the GTM motion producing results consistent with the company's MOVE stage?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: GTM covers full bow-tie — left side (Awareness, Education, Selection, Commitment) AND right side (Onboarding, Adoption, Impact, Expansion, Advocacy). MOVE stage-appropriate. Funnel metrics defined at each stage with conversion and velocity targets.
- **4**: Both sides covered but one side is thinner. Metrics defined but not tracked at every stage.
- **3**: Traditional acquisition funnel only. No expansion/retention strategy. GTM motion exists but not deliberately matched to buyer complexity.
- **2**: Ad-hoc GTM. No funnel architecture. No motion defined.
- **1**: No GTM strategy. Ship-and-hope.

## Dimension 5: Sales Enablement & Cross-Functional Readiness

**Proposal questions**: Does the enablement plan fulfill the Evangelist fundamental (LOVED)? Will the field be able to articulate the value proposition independently after training? Are battle cards, pitch decks, objection guides, and ROI calculators planned? Is there a readiness verification step before launch?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Were enablement assets delivered before launch? Was the sales team trained? Was readiness verified? Can reps articulate the value proposition independently?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Is the sales team using the enablement assets? Are they creating their own materials (a sign of enablement failure)? Are win rates improving? Is CS able to handle questions without escalation?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Full enablement suite deployed (deck, demo script, battle cards, objection guide, ROI calculator, one-pager per persona). Sales team trained and readiness verified before launch. CS and support briefed with documentation and macros. Partners enabled. Field articulates value independently.
- **4**: Most enablement assets delivered but one gap (e.g., no ROI calculator, or partners not briefed).
- **3**: Some enablement exists but incomplete. Sales team aware of the feature but not trained. No readiness verification.
- **2**: Minimal enablement. Sales team learns about the feature from customers or marketing materials not designed for sales use.
- **1**: No enablement. Sales team unaware of the change. LOVED Evangelist fundamental completely absent.

## Dimension 6: Launch Planning & Execution

**Proposal questions**: Is the launch deliberately tiered (T1/T2/T3)? Is there a launch brief with all standard sections? Are go/no-go criteria defined? Is Lauchengco's Release Scale applied? Are activities matched to the tier? Is a post-launch retrospective planned?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Was the launch tiered? Was a launch brief created? Was go/no-go conducted? Were activities appropriate for the tier?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Did the launch achieve its goals? Was a post-launch retrospective conducted? Are learnings feeding the next launch?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Launch deliberately tiered using Forrester model criteria. Launch brief with all standard sections (positioning, audience, objectives, pricing, distribution, enablement, CS plan, DACI). Go/no-go conducted 4-6 weeks pre-launch. Post-launch retrospective planned.
- **4**: Tiered and briefed but one area thin (e.g., no go/no-go decision point, or no post-launch retrospective).
- **3**: Some launch planning but no tiering framework. Activities are ad-hoc rather than matched to tier.
- **2**: Launch is a blog post. No coordination across functions.
- **1**: No launch plan. Feature shipped silently.

## Dimension 7: Market Performance Measurement

**Proposal questions**: Are metrics planned across both halves of the bow-tie? Is a win/loss program defined (beyond CRM codes)? Is marketing attribution defined (pipeline influence + pipeline creation)? Are leading and lagging indicators identified?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Were performance metrics defined before launch? Is a win/loss program operational? Is attribution in place?

**Implementation-Results questions**: What do the metrics show? What does win/loss analysis reveal about buyer decision drivers? Is marketing's pipeline contribution measurable? Are both acquisition and expansion metrics tracked?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Active win/loss program with buyer interviews (not just CRM codes). Clozd's 4 pillars in place: Leadership sponsorship, quality data collection, systematic analysis, adoption and action (monthly Voice of Buyer brief). Metrics tracked across both bow-tie halves — acquisition (pipeline generated, win rate, CAC) AND expansion (NRR, expansion revenue, logo retention). Marketing attribution model defined.
- **4**: Win/loss program exists but not fully mature (e.g., interviews conducted but not systematically analyzed, or expansion metrics not tracked).
- **3**: CRM win/loss data only (85% inaccurate per Clozd). Some pipeline metrics but no expansion measurement. No attribution model.
- **2**: Vanity metrics only (impressions, clicks, page views). No pipeline connection.
- **1**: No market performance measurement. Success declared without data.

## Dimension 8: Pricing & Packaging Strategy

**Proposal questions**: Is pricing market-informed (willingness-to-pay research such as Van Westendorp or Gabor-Granger)? Is there a competitive pricing benchmark? Does packaging create clear upgrade paths aligned with the bow-tie expansion model? Is the pricing strategy aligned with the GTM motion?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Was pricing researched before launch? Was competitive benchmarking conducted? Was packaging designed with expansion paths?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Is the pricing model producing expected results? Are upgrade paths being used? Is expansion revenue growing? Are there pricing-related objections in the sales process?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Willingness-to-pay research conducted. Competitive pricing benchmark completed. Packaging creates clear upgrade paths aligned with bow-tie expansion. Pricing aligns with GTM motion (e.g., usage-based for PLG, seat-based for sales-led). Value metric identified and validated.
- **4**: Research conducted but one area thin (e.g., no competitor benchmark, or upgrade paths unclear).
- **3**: Some pricing rationale but no formal research. Pricing based on cost-plus or gut feeling. Packaging exists but upgrade paths not designed.
- **2**: Pricing set by finance alone. No market input. No packaging strategy.
- **1**: No pricing strategy. Price is arbitrary or copied from a competitor without context.

## Dimension 9: Channel Strategy & Distribution

**Proposal questions**: Are channels selected based on where buyers actually consume information? Does the channel strategy match the product's adoption lifecycle stage (Moore)? Are channel economics modeled (CAC by channel)? Is there a content strategy mapped to buyer journey stages?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Were channels selected with buyer behavior data? Was the adoption lifecycle stage considered? Were channel economics modeled?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Are channels performing as expected? Is CAC by channel within tolerance? Are buyers being reached effectively? Is the content calendar producing pipeline?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Channels selected based on buyer behavior research. Channel economics modeled with CAC targets. Strategy matches adoption lifecycle stage (Moore). Content mapped to buyer journey stages. Channel performance tracked and optimized.
- **4**: Channels selected with some buyer input but economics not fully modeled. Some lifecycle stage awareness.
- **3**: Channels selected by convention ("everyone uses LinkedIn") rather than buyer behavior data. No economics. No lifecycle stage consideration.
- **2**: Spray across many channels with no rationale. No performance tracking.
- **1**: No channel strategy. Distribution is incidental.

## Dimension 10: Customer Evidence & Proof Points

**Proposal questions**: Is a customer evidence plan defined? Is there a Customer Discovery Program for identifying referenceable customers before broad launch? Are case studies planned with quantified results? Is there a reference customer recruitment strategy?

**Implementation-Compliance questions**: Were referenceable customers identified before launch? Were case studies created? Is there a proof point library? Was a Customer Discovery Program executed?

**Implementation-Results questions**: Are case studies being used in the sales process? Is the reference customer program active? Are proof points quantified and current? Is the proof point library growing?

**Scoring**:
- **5**: Customer Discovery Program executed — referenceable customers identified and onboarded before broad launch. Case studies with quantified results published. Reference customer program active with recruitment pipeline. Proof point library growing and accessible to sales. Analyst validation obtained.
- **4**: Some reference customers but program not systematic. Case studies exist but not all quantified. Proof points available but library not maintained.
- **3**: A few customer quotes but no structured program. No quantified case studies. Proof points are anecdotal.
- **2**: Claims made without customer backing. "Industry-leading" assertions with no evidence. No reference customers.
- **1**: No customer evidence. All claims are assertions. No proof point strategy.

## Dimension Weighting Guide

Default weighting is equal (all dimensions scored 1-5, summed to /50). Adjust emphasis by input type:

| Input Type | Higher Weight Dimensions | Lower Weight Dimensions |
|-----------|------------------------|------------------------|
| New Product Launch | Positioning, GTM Strategy, Market Understanding | Pricing (if free/beta) |
| Feature Update | Launch Planning, Sales Enablement, Customer Evidence | Channel Strategy (established) |
| Market Entry (new segment) | Market Understanding, Channel Strategy, Pricing | Launch Planning (iterative) |
| Competitive Response | Competitive Differentiation, Positioning, Sales Enablement | Launch Planning (speed) |
| Pricing Change | Pricing & Packaging, Competitive, Market Understanding | Launch Planning (if internal) |

Weighting adjusts emphasis in the rationale, not the scoring scale. All dimensions still receive a 1-5 score.

## Verdict Thresholds

### Proposal Reviews

| Verdict | Score Range | Meaning |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Proceed** | 42-50 | Strong across all dimensions; ready to take to market |
| **Proceed with Conditions** | 27-41 | Viable but gaps need addressing before or during launch |
| **Rework Required** | 0-26 | Fundamental gaps; return to market research |

### Implementation Reviews

| Verdict | Score Range | Meaning |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Meets Standards** | 42-50 | Well-executed; market outcomes being achieved |
| **Needs Improvement** | 27-41 | Functional but significant gaps to address |
| **Critical Gaps** | 0-26 | Fundamental issues; remediation plan needed |

### Critical Dimension Rules

Regardless of total score:
- Any dimension scoring 1 = cannot receive "Proceed" or "Meets Standards" verdict
- Positioning + Market Understanding both scoring 2 or below = automatic "Rework Required" / "Critical Gaps"
